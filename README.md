# Tableau-healthcare-business-insights

## Overview

This project explores public healthcare datasets from the United States, focusing on surgical operations (2019) and historical national health expenditures (1980–2018). The objective is to answer key business questions through interactive dashboards and data storytelling using Tableau.

The project combines analytical reasoning, visual design, and domain interpretation to provide actionable insights for healthcare decision-making.

## Objectives

The project addresses four key business questions:

1. Which surgical procedures exceed their planned duration, and why?
2. Are there physicians at risk of overwork based on workload and diversity of procedures?
3. Which healthcare departments show the highest cost variation over time?
4. Which categories should receive more or less investment?

---

## Methodology

### Data Visualization Approach

Each question is addressed through a dedicated dashboard, designed using appropriate visual encodings and interaction techniques:

* **Bar charts** → comparison of planned vs actual durations
* **Scatter plots** → workload vs diversity analysis
* **Line charts** → temporal trends and cost evolution
* **Normalized stacked bar charts** → budget distribution

The design follows principles of clarity, comparability, and storytelling.

---

## Key Insights

### Surgical Data

* Certain procedures consistently exceed planned durations, likely due to complexity and patient-specific factors.
* Some physicians handle both a high number and a wide variety of procedures, indicating potential overwork risk.

### Health Expenditures

* Total national health expenditures show the highest growth and variability over time.
* Budget allocation is heavily concentrated, with opportunities to optimize distribution across categories.
